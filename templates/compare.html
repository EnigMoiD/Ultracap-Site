Graphene Ultracapacitors

What They Are
Graphene ultracapacitors are...
Pros

Fuel cells

What They Are
Most fuel cells work by oxidizing a fuel like hydrogen, taking advantage of the electrical current generated in the process. Thus the byproduct of the chemical reaction is often simply water.
Pros
More energy dense than batteries, their fuel is renewable, nonpolluting, low maintenance, silent and safe. High electrical efficiency of 60-70% (3x internal combustion engines)
Cons
Too expensive and low power/ and low lifetime to compete with gas/combustion engines today, low durability (gas impurities and pulse demands shorten cell life), low power density by volume (10-100x less than combustion engines), does not allow bidirectional energy flow (no regen), slow start-up/power response/acceleration
Takeaway
Are being worked to replace batteries, ideal when an electrical supply is required, will be more commercially important when they can be manufactured more cheaply. It is better used as a steady source of energy and not a dynamic one: good in conjunction with a battery or ultracapacitor to provide steady energy and high power when needed.

Activated Carbon Ultracaps

What They Are
Ultracapacitors, like capacitors, generate energy through the buildup of opposing charges on the two capacitor plates. Ultracapacitors can have extremely high capacitances by increasing the surface area of the plates, which is done by coating the plates with activated carbon, a spongy and porous material. They are also called electrical double layer capacitors because each carbon electrode has two layers of charge coating its surface.
Pros
High power density, low internal resistance, much higher capacitance than a normal capacitor, more efficient than batteries, long life, more environmentally friendly than batteries
Cons
Relatively low energy density, low voltage means that ultracapacitorss have to be used in series to have the voltage of a battery, unsteady voltage because it drops as an ultracapactor discharges, fire hazard if the circuit shorts due to low internal resistance, trying to increase voltage/energy is expensive and leads to reduced power.
Takeaway
Their power density makes them great at meeting surge power demands on hybrid vehicles. When further developed, they may be able to store as much energy as batteries.

Batteries in general

What They Are
Batteries convert chemical energy into electrical energy. These devices are formed by sandwiching an electrolyte with a conductive anode and cathode. This creates a flow in electrons (a current) with associated electrical power. Cells can be recharged by introducing current, reversing the chemical reaction.
Pros
Instant power. No hassle. Easy to use and variable in size, temp, chemical system, orientation. Can provide pulses of high current for short periods of time as required.
Cons
Power discharge must be regulated to ensure health. Charging takes multiple hours. Low lifetime and number of cycles. Low energy content and expensive in comparison to traditional fuels.
Takeaway

Lithium ion

What They Are
Pros
Good at its job. High energy density and lighter than many other kinds, like the nickel metal hydride cell. Often rechargeable.
Cons
Risk of explosions and fires. Expensive. Lithium is difficult to mine and harmful to the environment.
Takeaway
Currently used in all modern electronic devices. Goto for high quality batteries. Widely used for portable electronics because of its light weight and energy density.

Lead Acid

What They Are
Pros
Easy to make. Reliable. Can store energy for a long period of time. Can supply very high surge currents. Cheap and has established recycling processes resulting in 98% of US lead acid batteries being recycled.
Cons
Lead is a toxic metal. Acids are dangerous. These batteries don't store energy very well.
Takeaway
Lead Acid batteries are mainly used in automobiles because of their ability to supply high current for the spark plugs while holding their charge for long periods of time to power minor systems. Their low energy density, high weight, and dangerous chemicals keep them out of most consumer devices.

Pumped water storage

What They Are
This system uses gravity to store energy. Water is pumped up a hill to a reservoir where it is stored for future use. To obtain power, this water is released and run through a generator.
Pros
Very efficient (up to 75%) Good for high energy amounts
Cons
Energy is not instantaneously available
Takeaway
great for powerplants. Used in powerplants already. Ultra large scale needed.
