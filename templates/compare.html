Graphene Ultracapacitors

What They Are
Graphene ultracapacitors are...
Pros
Relatively energy dense, very power dense, long lifespan, made from sustainable ingredients (carbon).
Cons
Only exist in research labs, expensive to produce.
Takeaway
Their combination of high power density, reasonable energy density, and long life make them well suited for a wide variety of applications ranging from consumer electronics due to their high energy content and quick charging rate to regenerative braking due to their efficiency and ability provide and store large amounts of power.
Sustainability
Graphene is quite a difficult material to produce. Some methods, such as chemical vapor deposition, are relatively clean processes with minimal hazards produced.Others, such as Hummer’s method, require many toxic acids and produce a lot of waste. Once the device itself is made, the materials in it are quite sustainable. The device can be used for extremely high cycle counts. When the capacitor is no longer useful, both the graphene and the electrolyte are biodegradable. [CITE ME]

Fuel cells

What They Are
Most fuel cells work by oxidizing a fuel like hydrogen, taking advantage of the electrical current generated in the process. Thus the byproduct of the chemical reaction is often simply water.
Pros
More energy dense than batteries, fuel is renewable not dependent on natural resources, nonpolluting, low maintenance, silent and safe, high electrical efficiency of 60-70% (3x internal combustion engines)
Cons
Too expensive and low power / low lifetime to compete with gas/combustion engines today, low durability, low power density (10-100x less than combustion engines), does not allow bidirectional energy flow, slow start-up/power response/acceleration
Takeaway
Are being worked to replace batteries, ideal when an electrical supply is required, will be more commercially important when they can be manufactured more cheaply. It is better used as a steady source of energy and not a dynamic one: good in conjunction with a battery or ultracap to provide steady energy
Sustainability
Fuel cells can run on a number of different fuel sources. A common “fuel” is water. Water gets converted to hydrogen and oxygen gas. Both of these chemicals occur naturally in nature. These fuel cells rely on the availability of hydrogen. In an ideal world, a nation would have a hydrogen economy where the distribution of hydrogen is stable.

Activated Carbon Ultracaps

What They Are
Ultracapacitors, like capacitors, generate energy through the buildup of opposing charges on the two capacitor plates. Ultracapacitors can have extremely high capacitances by increasing the surface area of the plates, which is done by coating the plates with activated carbon, which is spongy and porous. They are also called electrical double layer capacitors because each carbon electrode has two layers of charge coating its surface.
Pros
High power density, low internal resistance, much higher capacitance than a normal capacitor, more efficient than batteries, long life, more environmentally friendly than batteries
Cons
Relatively low energy density, low voltage means that ultracaps have to be used in series to have the voltage of a battery, unsteady voltage because it drops as an ultracap discharges, fire hazard if the circuit shorts due to low internal resistance, trying to increase voltage/energy is expensive and leads to reduced power
Takeaway
Their power density makes them great at meeting surge power demands on hybrid vehicles. When further developed, they may be able to store as much energy as batteries.
Sustainability
These devices are mostly made of carbon, thus are biodegradable. Theses devices also can be charged and discharged many times, thus they will not have to be replaced and reprocessed.

Gasoline

What They Are
In 1879, the first gasoline powered automobile was created. This spawned the paradigm shift in how people move around. Since then, gasoline has been used almost everywhere power is needed.
Pros
Very energy dense and powerful for its weight. Historically, gasoline has been fairly cheap.
Cons
Getting more and more expensive. Not sustainable. Creates greenhouse and toxic gasses.
Takeaway
Gasoline is not the future of energy storage both due to the remaining resources and the environmental impact. This technology will remain to be used while new technologies and products are developed.
Sustainability
Gasoline is terrible for the environment. Its consumption creates many greenhouse gases. In addition to this, the amount of oil on the earth is decreasing and prices are increasing.

Batteries in general

Batteries convert chemical energy into electrical energy. These devices are formed by sandwiching an electorate with a conductive anode and cathode. This creates a flow in electrons, creating electricity. When charging the cell, the reaction is reversed.
Pros
Instant power. No hassle. Easy to use and variable in size, temp, chemical system, orientation. Can provide pulses of high current
Cons
Power discharge must be regulated to ensure health. Charging takes multiple hours. Low lifetime. Low energy content and expensive in comparison to traditional fuels

Lithium ion

What They Are
Lithium ion batteries, much like all other battery technologies convert chemical energy into electrical energy. This device has 2 electrodes, (anode, and cathode) and an electrolyte. The anode of the battery is made of Lithium cobalt oxide, and the cathode is made of carbon. Li+ ions travel through the ion porous membrane and collect on the cathode when powering and reverses when charging.
Pros
Instant power. No hassle. Easy to use and variable in size, temp, chemical system, orientation. Can provide pulses of high current. High energy density and lighter than many other kinds, like the nickel metal hydride cell. Often rechargeable.
Cons
Power discharge must be regulated to ensure health. Charging takes multiple hours. Low lifetime. Low energy content and expensive in comparison to traditional fuels. Risk of explosions and fires. Expensive. Lithium hard to mine and bad for environment.
Takeaway
Currently used in all modern electronic devices. Goto for high quality batteries. Widely used for portable electronics
Sustainability

Lead Acid

What They Are
Pros
Instant power. No hassle. Easy to use and variable in size, temp, chemical system, orientation. Easy to make. Reliable. Can store energy for a long period of time. Can supply very high surge currents. Cheap and has established recycling processes resulting in 98% of US lead acid batteries being recycled.
Cons
Power discharge must be regulated to ensure health. Charging takes multiple hours. Low lifetime. Low energy content and expensive in comparison to traditional fuels. Lead is a toxic metal. Acids are dangerous. Do not operate well in low temperatures.
Takeaway
Lead Acid batteries are mainly used in automobiles because of their ability to supply high current for the spark plugs while holding their charge for long periods of time to power minor systems. Their low energy density, high weight, and dangerous chemicals keep them out of most consumer devices.
